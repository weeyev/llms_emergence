Final synthesis

Emergence evidence became strongest when we stopped asking for broad intelligence and started asking for compact causal structure.

The audit began as a broad search over claims that LLMs or neural systems show emergence. The final position is narrower and more scientific: some transformer systems show paper-defined emergent capabilities through coarse-grained internal variables, but the evidence does not establish broad emergent intelligence.

Final stance: benchmark jumps and sudden task scores are weak by themselves. The strongest evidence comes from function vectors, task vectors, world-state vectors, concept/spatial vectors, circuit-development trajectories, and progress measures that help predict, explain, or intervene on behavior.

Method Evolution

01

Broad search

Search space was intentionally wide: old and new papers, direct emergence claims, near-emergence mechanisms, controls, and counterclaims.

02

Rubric correction

The old linear gate was replaced. Scaling, criticality, compression, novel bases, and generalization became routes, not mandatory sequential gates.

03

Source checks

Original sources, newer versions, full texts, code, and criticism were checked where possible. Abstract-only matches stayed lower confidence.

04

Final criteria

Candidate papers were judged by emergence-selection criteria: compact structure, phase-like behavior, transfer, and knowledge-in mechanism.

05

Level 1 audit

The 18 strongest mechanistic papers were re-read for surprises and organized into a criteria matrix with evidence notes.

Final Selection Criteria

These are the criteria used to select and explain the strongest papers. They are different from the precursor checks such as source validity and method quality.

Scaling

Scale in data, compute, parameters, training time, or components reveals a new capability or organization.

Criticality

A rapid qualitative shift, threshold, phase-like transition, or order-parameter-like change is observed.

Compression

A higher-level compressed description remains useful while ignoring lower-level microscopic detail.

Novel bases

A new representational basis, subspace, manifold, internal alphabet, or geometry improves representation.

Generalization

The structure supports transfer, analogy, OOD behavior, rapid adaptation, or reuse beyond the exact setup.

Knowledge-in mechanism

The global property is connected to an internal/local neural mechanism, not only to behavior.

Capability vs intelligence

The paper is clear about narrow emergent capability versus broad emergent intelligence.

Controls and lineage

114 effect/counterclaim papers and 163 claim-source-only papers were retained. These matter because many emergence claims are weakened by metric artifacts, memorization, prompting, contamination, ordinary scaling, or shortcut heuristics.

Clean-up

Superseded linear-gate reports were removed. Raw PDFs/text and final V2 artifacts were kept so future reading starts from the corrected paper-grounded rubric.

Guth, Menard, and Rainbow

Exact Guth/Menard 2025 claim

Unverifiable In prep / personal communication Double descent claim

The target emergence paper says Guth and Menard 2025 show that the double-descent peak coincides with covariance spectra moving from exponential to scale-free. The local arXiv target paper calls it in preparation; the Royal Society version calls it personal communication.

Verdict: do not count the exact double-descent/scale-free-transition claim as verified until a public manuscript, data, or code appears.

Public Rainbow JMLR paper

Released Compression route Novel-basis route Not LLM evidence

A Rainbow in Deep Network Black Boxes is real public evidence for covariance-based coarse-graining, low-rank eigenspaces, and sampled rainbow networks preserving performance in scoped CNN/scattering settings.

Verdict: use as high-quality adjacent evidence for compression and novel bases, not as verification of the exact unpublished double-descent claim.

Cluster Findings

Function vectors became the strongest LLM-positive route

Function-vector papers converge on compact internal vectors/subspaces that can trigger or steer task behavior. This is the strongest match to compression plus knowledge-in mechanism.

Induction heads are developmental, not the whole story

Several papers suggest induction heads may seed later function-vector mechanisms, and attention-sink formation can occur on a different timetable.

World models are real in controlled domains

Othello, MetaOthello, chess, and spatial datasets show editable internal state variables, but the evidence remains formal-domain and contested.

Grokking is both positive and deflationary

Progress measures give a strong internal mechanism, but also show that a behavioral jump can hide gradual circuit formation.

Final warning: the audit supports some paper-defined emergent capabilities. It does not support the broad claim that LLMs have emergent intelligence.
